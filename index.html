Planning Checklist

Fall = Japan's most competitive travel window. Late October–November foliage season fills ryokans, omakase counters, and boutique hotels months in advance. Complete Phase 1 before you finalize your flights.

Flights — SAN → NRT (Tokyo Narita), round-trip
JAL and ANA fly direct SAN→NRT (~11hrs). JAL opens 360 days ahead, ANA 355. Round-trip into/out of Tokyo eliminates backtracking from Osaka at the end.
Search flights ↗
JR Pass — 21-day, buy before leaving the US
Cheaper abroad. Covers all Shinkansen (Hikari/Sakura, not Nozomi) + regional JR lines. Activate on Day 1 at Narita. Key routes: Tokyo↔Hakone, Tokyo→Kyoto (2h15m), Kyoto↔Osaka, Osaka→Tokyo for departure.
Buy JR Pass ↗
Hakone Ryokan — private onsen room, 1 night
Fall foliage demand is extreme. Private rotenburo rooms go months ahead. Look in Gora or Miyagino. Dinner-inclusive plans (~¥30,000/person) strongly recommended — avoids the evening restaurant hunt in a mountain town. Mt. Fuji views weather permitting.

Ghibli Museum — mandatory advance tickets
No walk-ins. Ever. Buy 3 months ahead via Lawson or the international portal. Sells out same day tickets open.

Kinosaki Ryokan — weeks/months ahead in peak season
The strongest differentiator of the whole trip. Book before any Tokyo hotel.

Omakase Sushi — Tokyo counter, pre-book 6+ months
The best counters are fully booked 6+ months ahead. Use Tableall (paid concierge, ¥8,000/seat fee — worth it for the hardest spots), Ikyu, or hotel concierge. Target ¥15,000–25,000/person — extraordinary value for the quality level.

Kaiseki Dinner — Kyoto, book 3–6 months ahead
Kyoto is the home of kaiseki — multi-course seasonal Japanese tasting menu. ¥12,000–20,000/person at a respected mid-range spot will be one of the best restaurant experiences of your life. Use Tablecheck, Tableall, or Ikyu.

TeamLab Borderless — Tokyo, timed-entry tickets
Reopened 2024 at Azabudai Hills. Genuinely extraordinary — immersive, nothing else like it. Book directly from official site. Earliest weekday slot to avoid crowds. Allow 3+ hours inside.

Shibuya Sky — rooftop observation deck
Open-air rooftop. The Scramble crossing from above at magic hour, then at night, is genuinely extraordinary. Book online to skip the queue.

Shinkansen Seat Reservations
JR reservations open exactly 1 month before travel at 10am JST. Use SmartEX app (set up before you leave) or reserve at JR ticket offices after arrival. Key: Tokyo→Kyoto, Kyoto→Osaka, Osaka→Tokyo (for departure).

Live House Show Tickets
Check venue Instagram accounts and Resident Advisor Japan 1–2 weeks out. Buy via LivePocket, e+, or Pia. @shinjukuloft, @womb_tokyo, @gattaca_kyoto, @conpass_osaka for upcoming shows. Many shows have door spots but advance saves ~¥500. Small rooms sell out.
RA Japan ↗
Download Offline Maps + Apps
Before flying: offline Google Maps for Tokyo, Kyoto, and Osaka; Japanese translation pack; DeepL; Hyperdia or Jorudan (train times); Tabelog; GO app for late-night taxis. Set up SmartEX for Shinkansen reservations.
Notify Bank + Pack Cash
Notify cards you'll be in Japan. Japan is still very cash-heavy — live houses, record shops, vintage stores, small ramen counters. Carry ¥30,000–50,000 at all times. ATMs at 7-Eleven and Japan Post reliably accept foreign cards.
Yamato Luggage Forwarding — plan for vinyl + shopping
Yamato "takkyubin" ships bags between hotels overnight (~¥1,500–2,500/bag) or direct to international addresses. Essential if you buy records, model kits, or clothes. Set up at any convenience store or hotel front desk — no account needed.

Trip logistics · Links · Day trips

Practical Info

Full Route
Tokyo I (Days 1–6) → Hakone overnight (Day 7) → Kyoto (Days 8–11) → Kinosaki (Days 12–13) → Osaka (Days 14–16) → Tokyo II (Day 17) → fly home from NRT
Getting Around
21-day JR Pass for all Shinkansen. Suica for subways + daily life. GO app for late-night taxis. Walk Shimokitazawa, Koenji, Nakameguro. Rent a car only for Daikoku Futo PA run.
Budget
~$150–200/day covers accommodation, food, entry fees, and some shopping. Live houses: ¥2,000–4,000 + drink. Records/models: budget what your bags can handle. Convenience stores (7-Eleven, FamilyMart) are genuinely great for cheap breakfasts.
Foliage (if Spring)
Cherry blossom typically peaks late March–early April in Kyoto. Check Japan Guide's forecast (updated in real time each season). Maruyama Park, Philosopher's Path, and Arashiyama are the classic Kyoto spots.
Music + Records
RA Japan for live lineups · Disk Union multi-floor · Jet Set (house/techno) · Flash Disc Ranch · Big Love Records (Harajuku, IPA on tap)
Coffee Circuit
Onibus (Nakameguro) · Koffee Mameya (Omotesando) · Fuglen (Tomigaya) · Bear Pond (Shimokitazawa) · Weekenders (Kyoto, weekends only) · Kaikado Café (Kyoto, 150-year workshop) · Graph (Osaka)
Key Booking Links
Tabelog (restaurants) · Tableall (hard-to-book counters) · Hyperdia (trains) · SmartEX (Shinkansen reservations) · 21_21 Design Sight · Mori Art Museum
Luggage + Shipping
Yamato takkyubin ships bags between hotels overnight (~¥1,500–2,500/bag) or direct to international addresses. Essential for vinyl and model kit hauls. Set up at any convenience store — no account needed. Narita has a tax refund counter for large purchases.
Day Trips Worth Taking
From Tokyo
Nikko
~2hrs by JR from Shinjuku · Covered by JR Pass
Insanely ornate Toshogu Shrine buried in cedar forests and fall foliage. The gilded lacquerwork feels almost delirious. Best mid-November for peak red maples. Combine with Chuzenji Lake. Highly recommended swap if you want a quieter day. Plan your visit →
From Tokyo
Kamakura
~1hr by JR from Shinjuku · Covered by JR Pass
The Great Buddha. Sea-facing temples. Bamboo groves without the Arashiyama crowds. The coastal Enoden tram is one of the best short train rides in Japan. Strong café culture near Hase station. Good swap for a day if Tokyo museum fatigue sets in. Plan your visit →
From Osaka / Kyoto
Nara
45min from Kyoto or Osaka · Kintetsu Line
Todai-ji (one of the largest wooden structures on Earth) + 1,200 freely roaming deer that will headbutt you for crackers. Naramachi backstreet cafés are genuinely charming. Kasuga Taisha Shrine with hundreds of stone lanterns lining a forested path. Already on Day 14 of the itinerary — swap to this tab for the links. Plan your visit →
From Kyoto / En Route
Toyota Museum
Nagoya stop · 40min Shinkansen from Kyoto
Best as an optional stop en route between Tokyo and Kyoto. The Toyota Automobile Museum in Nagoya is world-class for JDM history — production Celicas, Supras, genuine 2000GTs, full racing history. Allow 3–4 hours. Check Shinkansen schedule for a convenient stopover day. Museum info →
